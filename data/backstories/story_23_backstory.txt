Hildred Castaigne began as a rational man, trained to value logic and order. Educated, confident, and intellectually ambitious, he believed knowledge elevated humanity. Yet his pride made him careless.

Exposure to forbidden ideas did not corrupt him instantly—it appealed to his ego. The play promised insight beyond ordinary minds. Hildred did not fear madness; he feared insignificance. His descent is gradual, driven by obsession rather than terror.

Hildred’s backstory is a warning: intellect without humility invites destruction.
